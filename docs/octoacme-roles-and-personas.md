# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They serve the team by fostering self-organization and continuous improvement while protecting the team from distractions.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Track and remove blockers and impediments to team progress
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external interruptions and scope changes
- Monitor team health, velocity, and process adherence
- Facilitate collaboration between Product Managers, Developers, and stakeholders

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure agile ceremonies are effective and time-efficient
- Maintain high team morale and psychological safety

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and real-time blocker resolution
- Sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retrospectives)
- One-on-one coaching with team members
- Collaboration with Project Managers on risks and dependencies

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes impediments, facilitates collaboration
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on risks, dependencies, and cross-team issues
- **UX Designers**: Ensures design work is integrated into sprint planning
- **DevOps Engineers**: Works to resolve deployment blockers and process improvements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, create design solutions, and validate usability to ensure products are intuitive and delightful. They work closely with Product Managers and Developers to translate user insights into effective interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics
- Maintain design systems and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with technical and business constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Handoff meetings with Developers
- User research findings presentations
- Collaboration with Product Managers on feature definition

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on problem definition and feature requirements
- **Developers**: Partners on implementation, provides design specs and assets
- **Scrum Master**: Coordinates sprint planning to ensure design readiness
- **Stakeholders**: Presents research findings and design rationale

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They ensure reliable, secure, and efficient software delivery while improving developer productivity through tooling and automation.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure, deployment environments, and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and observability solutions
- Ensure security scanning and compliance in deployment pipelines
- Support incident response and production troubleshooting
- Optimize build times and deployment processes

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, automated deployments
- Minimize deployment failures and downtime
- Improve developer experience and productivity
- Maintain infrastructure reliability and security

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and release coordination
- Incident response and postmortems
- Infrastructure and tooling improvement proposals
- Documentation of deployment procedures and runbooks

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides tooling, CI/CD support, and deployment guidance
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates release schedules and deployment windows
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on removing deployment blockers
- **Stakeholders**: Reports on system health, uptime, and incident impact

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with vested interest in project outcomes, including executives, customers, partners, and cross-functional leaders. They provide strategic input, approve decisions, and receive project updates to ensure alignment with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and strategic direction
- Review and approve project scope, budget, and timeline
- Participate in key decision points and milestone reviews
- Offer feedback on demos and deliverables
- Champion the project within their organization or domain
- Escalate concerns and risks to appropriate levels

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver expected business value
- Maintain alignment with organizational strategy
- Manage resource allocation and priorities
- Mitigate business and reputational risks

### Typical Communication
- Monthly status updates and milestone reviews
- Demos and release announcements
- Decision requests and approval workflows
- Escalation discussions for critical issues

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provides requirements, approves scope and priorities
- **Project Managers**: Receives status updates, helps resolve escalations
- **Developers**: Reviews deliverables during demos and milestone reviews
- **Scrum Master**: Minimal direct interaction; may observe reviews or retrospectives
- **UX Designer**: Provides feedback on user experience and design decisions
- **DevOps Engineer**: Informed of production incidents and system health
